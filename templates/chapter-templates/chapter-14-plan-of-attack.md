# Chapter 14: Plan of Attack

## Chapter Purpose and Narrative Function
The hero and their allies formulate their strategy for confronting the main problem. This chapter focuses on preparation, strategy, and team coordination. It shows the hero in a leadership role and sets up the specific challenges and obstacles that will be faced in the coming climactic sequence.

## Key Beats to Hit
- Assessment of the challenge and available resources
- Strategic planning session with key allies
- Assignment of roles and responsibilities
- Identification of potential obstacles and contingency plans
- Hero demonstrates leadership and strategic thinking
- Team bonding and motivation before the big push
- End with the plan set and the team ready to execute

## Character State/Arc Progression
- **Beginning**: Hero is morally centered after the mirror stage
- **End**: Hero is prepared strategically and emotionally for the final challenges
- **Internal Arc**: From individual hero to team leader and strategist
- **Skills/Abilities**: Integration of all learned skills into a comprehensive approach
- **Relationships**: Full team coordination and mutual trust

## Conflict Escalation Requirements
- **Level**: Strategic/Preparation (6-7 out of 10)
- **Type**: Planning challenges, resource limitations, tactical decisions
- **Stakes**: The effectiveness of the coming confrontation
- **Tension**: Build through the magnitude of what they're planning to attempt
- **Opponent**: The seeming impossibility of success, time pressure

## Character Development Focus
- Show the hero's growth into a leader and strategist
- Demonstrate their ability to coordinate and inspire others
- Reveal their tactical intelligence and problem-solving skills
- Display their willingness to put others in danger for the greater good
- Test their ability to balance confidence with realistic assessment

## Typical Scene Elements
- War council or strategy meeting
- Reconnaissance or intelligence gathering
- Resource allocation and preparation montages
- Individual conversations with key team members
- Study of maps, plans, or enemy intelligence
- Training or rehearsal of specific tactics
- Moment of doubt balanced by determination

## Word Count Target
**3,000-4,000 words**

## Writing Notes
- Balance detailed planning with character development
- Use strategy sessions to reveal character relationships and dynamics
- Show the hero's growth through their leadership approach
- Make the plan specific enough to create anticipation for execution
- Include realistic obstacles and limitations to maintain tension
- Build excitement for the coming action while maintaining uncertainty about success
